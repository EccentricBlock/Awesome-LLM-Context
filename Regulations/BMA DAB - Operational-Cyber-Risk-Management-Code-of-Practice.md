## 4.1 Board Level Governance of Cyber Risk

### Overview
The board of directors and senior management must oversee cyber risks. They are responsible for approving a cyber risk policy document, either as a standalone or as part of a broader risk policy, at least annually. Updates on the cyber risk status must be provided regularly to the board and senior management.

### Key Points
- **Board Oversight:** Board and senior management must approve the cyber risk policy.
- **Policy Requirements:** Can be a standalone policy or a section in an operational risk policy.
- **Reporting:** Regular updates on cyber risk status must be provided to the board.

### Requirements
- The cyber risk policy must be approved by the board annually.
- Senior management must receive regular updates regarding cyber risk.

## 4.2 The Role of the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)

### Overview
The role of the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) must be allocated to a qualified internal or external resource. If outsourced, oversight remains with the board. The CISO is responsible for delivering the operational cyber risk management programme.

### Key Points
- **CISO Appointment:** A qualified individual or outsourced resource.
- **Responsibility:** Oversight remains with the board even if the role is outsourced.
- **Operational Role:** CISO delivers the operational cyber risk management programme.

### Requirements
- The CISO must be a qualified individual, whether internal or outsourced.
- The CISO must have sufficient seniority to facilitate programme delivery.

## 4.3 The Operational Cyber Risk Management Programme

### Overview
The cyber risk policy objectives must be fulfilled through an operational cyber risk management programme. This programme should identify, evaluate, and manage cyber risks while also incorporating data governance, classification controls, and information security measures.

### Key Points
- **Risk Assessment:** Programme must include a process to identify, evaluate, and manage cyber risks.
- **Data Governance:** Policies for data classification and information security controls are required.
- **Detection and Response:** Programme should cover detection, protection, response, and recovery.

### Requirements
- Establish a documented cyber risk management programme.
- Employ personnel with adequate skills and knowledge of cybersecurity threats.
- Include detection, protection, response, and recovery controls.

## 4.4 Three Lines of Defense Model (3LOD)

### Overview
The Bermuda Monetary Authority requires digital asset businesses to adopt a Three Lines of Defense (3LOD) model for cyber risk governance. This model encompasses operational management, risk management, and audit.

### Key Points
- **Three Lines of Defense:** Model includes operational management, risk management, and audit.
- **Risk Governance:** Clear delineation of responsibilities among operational teams, risk management, and audit.

### Requirements
- Cyber risk governance must align with the 3LOD model.

## 4.5 Risk Assessment Process

### Overview
The risk assessment process is essential for understanding and managing cyber risks in digital asset businesses. It involves identifying potential risks to assets and individuals, measuring their impact, determining an appropriate response, and ensuring ongoing monitoring and documentation.

### Key Points
- **Identification:** Understand the potential cyber risks to operations, assets, and individuals.
- **Measurement:** Assess the consequences and impact of identified risks.
- **Response Planning:** Decide on risk responses based on asset criticality and risk tolerance.
- **Monitoring and Reporting:** Maintain a risk register for documenting risks and tracking them over time.

### Requirements
- A documented risk assessment process.
- Maintain and retain risk assessments for a minimum of five years.
- Regular monitoring and updating of the risk register.

## 4.9 Assets Identification

### Overview
Digital asset businesses must maintain an inventory of all information assets. Each asset must be classified by its value, legal requirements, sensitivity, and criticality. Information owners are responsible for classification and ensuring that appropriate protective controls are in place.

### Key Points

- **Ownership and Classification:** Designated business units must own information assets and classify them.
- **Protective Controls:** Classification determines appropriate protective controls based on sensitivity and criticality.

### Requirements

## 4.10 Managing Outsourcing and Third-Party Service Provider Cyber Risk

### Overview
When digital asset businesses (DABs) outsource cyber-related functions to external third parties or internal affiliated entities, they must maintain oversight and accountability for these functions. The service agreement must include compliance with laws, cooperation with the Bermuda Monetary Authority (BMA), and access to records.

### Key Points
- **Oversight Responsibility:** The DAB retains accountability even if cyber functions are outsourced.
- **Service Agreement:** Must include compliance clauses, cooperation with the Authority, and data access.
- **Risk Assessment:** Conduct a risk assessment before using third-party blockchain applications or services.
- **Governance Standards:** Maintain governance and internal controls equivalent to internal functions.

### Requirements
- Document and ensure oversight of outsourced functions.
- Complete risk assessments for third-party services.
- Include compliance clauses in service agreements.

## 5.6 Notification of Cyber Reporting Events to the Authority

### Overview
Digital Asset Businesses (DABs) must have policies and procedures to address the reporting of any cyber incidents or suspicious events to the Bermuda Monetary Authority (BMA). This includes cases such as hacks, thefts, or other attacks affecting the security of digital assets.

### Key Points
- **Reporting Requirements:** DABs must notify the Authority within 24 hours of determining a cyber incident.
- **Regular Updates:** Following the initial notification, updates must be provided until the incident is fully remediated.
- **Incident Reports:** A detailed incident report must be submitted within 14 days, summarizing the root cause, impact, and actions taken.

### Requirements
- Document and maintain policies for reporting cyber events.
- Notify the Authority within 24 hours of a significant cyber event.
- Submit an incident report within 14 days of notification.

- **Segregation of Duties:** Segregate roles and responsibilities to minimize misuse or abuse.
- **Audit Logs:** Maintain audit logs of access changes for at least five years.

### Requirements
- Establish access control procedures for all users.
- Maintain audit logs for all changes in access rights.

- **Mitigating Controls:** Implement appropriate mitigating controls for potential data breaches or exfiltration.

### Requirements
- Perform DLP requirement assessments for primary business applications.
- Implement appropriate DLP controls for classified data.

## 6.1 Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery Planning
### Overview
Digital Asset Businesses (DABs) must implement effective Business Continuity Planning (BCP) and Disaster Recovery (DR) policies and procedures to ensure that business services can continue during incidents and quickly recover from disruptions. Regular analysis, testing, and documentation are critical for maintaining effective continuity measures.

### Key Points
- **Business Impact Analysis (BIA):** Conduct documented BIAs to determine the criticality of business processes, assess recovery needs, and understand the impact of different disaster scenarios.
- **BCP and DR Plans:** Develop and maintain documented BCP and DR plans that are tested at least annually.
- **Testing and Remediation:** Document BCP and DR tests and track any issues identified during testing to ensure they are remediated effectively.

### Requirements
- Perform regular BIAs to evaluate business process criticality.
- Develop, document, and maintain BCP and DR plans.
- Test BCP and DR plans annually and remediate identified issues.
